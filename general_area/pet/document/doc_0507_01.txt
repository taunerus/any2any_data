ANIMAL NAME: Lion

SCIENTIFIC NAME: Panthera leo

LIFESPAN: 12

HABITAT: The natural habitat of the lion is in sub-Saharan Africa and in Asia, with an endangered population also living in a part of India. They inhabit savannas, grasslands, and woodlands, and are typically found in areas with widely scattered trees and distinct dry and rainy seasons.

DIET: Lions typically eat a variety of prey, ranging from small to large-sized ungulates (hoofed animals). Their primary diet consists of medium to large-sized ungulates, such as zebra, wildebeest, buffalo, gazelle, impala, and giraffe. They also hunt smaller prey like rodents and have been known to feed on seals along the coast in some regions. In addition, lions will scavenge and eat carrion if they come across it. The availability and types of prey can change with the seasons, and lions have been observed to adjust their diet accordingly.

FOOD CHAIN ROLE: Carnivore

ADAPTATION: Some adaptations that help lions survive in their environment include:

1. **Social structure**: Living in groups, known as prides, allows lions to hunt and protect their territory more effectively.
2. **Hunting strategies**: Lions use coordinated efforts to surround and take down prey, increasing their success rate.
3. **Adaptation to prey availability**: Lions adjust their diet based on the availability of prey, hunting smaller prey when larger prey is scarce.
4. **Scavenging**: Lions will scavenge for food if they come across a carcass killed by another predator.
5. **Roaring**: Lions use their roar to communicate with other group members and warn intruders of territorial boundaries.
6. **Retractable claws**: Lions have long, retractable claws that help them grab and hold prey.
7. **Rough tongue**: Lions' rough tongues help them peel the skin of prey animals away from flesh and flesh away from bone.
8. **Nocturnal behavior**: Lions are primarily nocturnal, which helps them hunt and avoid predators.
9. **Patience and stealth**: Lions are incredibly patient and good at hiding, allowing them to stalk and ambush prey.
10. **Strong legs and sharp claws**: Lions have powerful legs and sharp claws, which enable them to chase and catch prey.

These adaptations enable lions to thrive in their environment and play a crucial role in their survival.

ADAPTATION SUMMARY: Social, Hunting, Scavenging, Roaring, Stealth
