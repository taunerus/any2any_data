ANIMAL NAME: Gorilla

SCIENTIFIC NAME: Gorilla gorilla

LIFESPAN: 35-40

HABITAT: The natural habitat of the gorilla includes tropical forests in West and Central Africa. Specifically, the western lowland gorilla inhabits the tropical forests of Cameroon, Central African Republic, Gabon, Congo, and Equatorial Guinea, while the eastern lowland gorilla inhabits the tropical forests of Eastern Democratic Republic of the Congo. The mountain gorilla is found in the high-altitude tropical forests of the Virunga Volcano range, spanning across Zaire, Rwanda, and Uganda. Additionally, the Cross River gorilla inhabits a small area between Nigeria and Cameroon. These habitats provide the necessary environment for gorillas to thrive, with abundant food, shelter, and space to roam.

DIET: A gorilla's diet typically consists of vegetation, including leaves, stems, roots, vines, herbs, trees, and grasses. The specific composition of their diet varies by subspecies, but it generally includes:

* Western lowland gorillas: 67% fruit, 17% leaves, seeds, and stems, and 3% termites and caterpillars
* Eastern lowland gorillas: parts of at least 104 plant species
* Mountain gorillas: 86% leaves, shoots, and stems, 7% roots, 3% flowers, 2% fruit, and 2% ants, snails, and grubs
* Cross River gorillas: fruit, leaves, stems, piths, and some invertebrates

Gorillas are selective foragers, using their agile lips and hand dexterity to manipulate vegetation and consume specific parts of plants. They do not overexploit an area for food, instead cropping vegetation to allow for quick replenishment.

FOOD CHAIN ROLE: Omnivore

ADAPTATION: Some adaptations that help gorillas survive in their environment include:

1. **Thick fur**: Mountain gorillas have longer and thicker fur than lowland gorillas, which helps them adapt to the colder climate of their high-altitude habitats.
2. **Herbivorous diet**: Gorillas are able to survive on a diet of leaves, shoots, stems, and other vegetation, which is abundant in their forest habitats.
3. **Strong arms and hands**: Gorillas have powerful arms and hands that allow them to climb, brachiate, and manipulate food and other objects in their environment.
4. **Social structure**: Gorillas live in groups, called troops, which provide protection, social support, and cooperation in finding food and raising young.
5. **Adaptation to high altitudes**: Mountain gorillas have adapted to the low oxygen levels at high altitudes, allowing them to survive in areas that would be challenging for other great apes.
6. **Ability to eat low-nutrient food**: Gorillas are able to eat leaves, stems, and other low-nutrient food sources, which allows them to survive in areas where other food sources may be scarce.
7. **Expressive faces**: Gorillas have expressive faces that help them communicate with each other and convey emotions, which is important for their social behavior and survival.
8. **Strong sense of smell**: Gorillas have a strong sense of smell that helps them detect food, predators, and other important stimuli in their environment.

These adaptations enable gorillas to thrive in their environments, from the tropical forests of West Africa to the mountainous regions of central Africa.
