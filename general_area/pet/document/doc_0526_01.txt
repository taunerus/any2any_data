ANIMAL NAME: Tiger

SCIENTIFIC NAME: Panthera tigris

LIFESPAN: 12.5

HABITAT: The natural habitat of the tiger includes forests and grasslands in Asia, specifically in countries such as India, Russia, Bangladesh, Bhutan, China, Indonesia, Malaysia, Myanmar, Nepal, and Thailand. They occupy a much smaller territory than they did a century ago, having lost around 95% of their historical habitat. Tigers can be found in various types of habitats, including mangrove forests, tropical forests, and mountainous regions. They require large areas of habitat with adequate prey, water, and shelter to survive.

DIET: Tigers typically eat deer and other small to medium-sized ungulates. They are carnivores and primarily feed on large ungulates, such as deer and wild boar. According to the sources, it is estimated that every tiger consumes about 50 deer-sized animals each year, which translates to approximately one per week.

FOOD CHAIN ROLE: Carnivore

ADAPTATION: Some adaptations that help the Tiger survive in its environment include:

1. **Stripes for camouflage**: Tigers have distinctive striping on their hair and skin, which helps them blend in with their surroundings and provides camouflage.
2. **Guard hair and underfur for warmth and protection**: Tigers have two types of hair, guard hair and underfur, which provide warmth and protection from the elements.
3. **Specialized tongue for eating**: The tiger's tongue is covered with small, sharp projections called papillae, which help strip feathers, fur, and meat from prey.
4. **Powerful legs and sharp claws for hunting**: Tigers have strong legs and sharp claws, which enable them to chase and catch prey.
5. **Excellent night vision**: Tigers have large eyes and a reflective layer in the back of their eyes, which helps them see in low light conditions.
6. **Agile body for climbing and jumping**: Tigers have a flexible spine and powerful muscles, which allow them to climb trees and jump long distances.
7. **Tail for balance and communication**: The tiger's tail helps with balance while making sharp turns, and also plays a role in visual communication.
8. **Specialized stomach for digesting meat**: As a carnivore, the tiger's stomach is adapted to digest large amounts of meat, with a short digestive tract and high concentration of stomach acid.
9. **Unique stripe pattern for identification**: Each tiger's stripe pattern is unique, like a fingerprint, which helps with identification and recognition.
10. **Ability to adapt to different habitats**: Tigers can be found in a variety of habitats, from forests to grasslands, and have adapted to survive in different environments.
