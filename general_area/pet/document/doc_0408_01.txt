ANIMAL NAME: Dolphin

SCIENTIFIC NAME: Delphinidae

LIFESPAN: 40

HABITAT: Dolphins can be found across the globe in both tropical and temperate waters. They are unlikely to be found in the oceans around the Arctic and Antarctic, as they prefer warmer waters. The bottlenose dolphin, one of the most widely known species, thrives in water temperatures between 50 and 90 degrees Fahrenheit. Dolphins can be found in various locations, including the Mediterranean Sea, where they coexist with other species like Risso's dolphins, short-beaked common dolphins, and striped dolphins. However, dolphin populations in the Mediterranean Sea are rapidly diminishing due to various threats.

DIET: Dolphins typically eat fish, squid, and crustaceans. They are carnivores and their diet varies depending on the species and location. Bottlenose dolphins, for example, have been known to eat a variety of fish, including mullet, herring, and mackerel, as well as squid and crustaceans like shrimp and crabs. They have also been observed using various hunting techniques, such as coral reef fishing, cooperative hunting, and strand feeding, to catch their prey.

FOOD CHAIN ROLE: Carnivore

ADAPTATION: Dolphins have several adaptations that help them survive in their environment. 

1. **Streamlined body shape**: Dolphins have a fusiform body shape, which reduces drag and allows them to swim efficiently through the water.
2. **Blubber**: Dolphins have a thick layer of blubber that helps to conserve heat and reduce drag.
3. **Flippers and fins**: Dolphins have pectoral fins that act like steering wheels, helping them turn and change direction, and a dorsal fin that helps them maintain stability while swimming.
4. **Powerful tail**: Dolphins have a powerful tail that propels them through the water.
5. **Thermoregulatory strategies**: Dolphins have several thermoregulatory strategies to retain or release heat, including a decreased surface-to-volume ratio, increased insulation, and a heat exchange system.
6. **Efficient swimming**: Dolphins are among the world's most efficient swimmers, with a characteristic fusiform shape that creates less drag.
7. **Ability to hold breath**: Dolphins have a high myoglobin concentration in their muscles, which allows them to conserve oxygen while diving.
8. **Sensory adaptations**: Dolphins have sensitive skin, good eyesight, and a sense of electroreception, which helps them detect prey and navigate their environment.
9. **Social behavior**: Dolphins are highly social animals that often cooperate when hunting and catching fish, and have been observed using tools to help them forage for food.
10. **Adaptability**: Dolphins are able to adapt to different habitats and environments, and have been found in both tropical and temperate waters around the world.

ADAPTATION SUMMARY: Streamlined, Blubber, Flippers, Thermoregulation, Efficiency
