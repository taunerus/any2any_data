ANIMAL NAME: Elephant

SCIENTIFIC NAME: Loxodonta africana and Elephas maximus

LIFESPAN: 70

HABITAT: The natural habitat of elephants varies depending on the species, but they are generally found in savannas, forests, and grasslands in Africa and Asia. African elephants are typically found in savannas, grasslands, and forests in sub-Saharan Africa, while Asian elephants are found in forests and grasslands in southern and southeastern Asia. 

Elephants migrate and generally follow the same migratory routes annually, with migration distances varying considerably depending on environmental conditions. During a prolonged dry season in Africa, elephant migration distances can extend over 100 km, while in Asian deciduous forests, migration distances can range from 20 to 50 km.

Elephants inhabit areas with abundant food and water sources, and their habitats are often characterized by grasslands, savannas, and forests with seasonal droughts, occasional fires, and grazing by large mammals. The soil in these habitats is often nutrient-rich, and perennial grass roots are deeply embedded.

Human activities, such as agriculture and urbanization, have led to habitat fragmentation, reducing breeding opportunities and limiting genetic variability. As a result, elephant populations are often compressed into smaller areas, leading to over-exploitation and damage to their habitats.

DIET: Elephants typically eat a variety of plants, including tree bark, which contains calcium and roughage to aid digestion. They also dig up earth to obtain salt and minerals. In addition, they consume crops, such as oil palm and rubber, and can eat and trample down large areas of cultivated fields in a single night. In captivity, elephants are often fed hay, with an adult elephant consuming 4-5 bales of hay per day.

FOOD CHAIN ROLE: Herbivore.

ADAPTATION: Some adaptations that help elephants survive in their environment include:

1. **Trunk**: A versatile and multi-purpose organ used for breathing, drinking, eating, grasping, and social interactions.
2. **Tusks**: Used for defense, digging, and lifting, and are made of ivory.
3. **Large ears**: Help to radiate excess heat and keep the elephant cool in hot climates.
4. **Thick skin**: Protects the elephant from the elements and provides insulation.
5. **Pilose skin**: Covers the elephant's body, with hairs that help to sense the environment.
6. **Specialized feet**: With five toes and a thick, elastic pad that helps to distribute weight and provides traction.
7. **Dietary adaptations**: Elephants are herbivores and have a specialized digestive system that allows them to break down and extract nutrients from plant material.
8. **Migration patterns**: Elephants migrate to find food, water, and suitable habitats, and have been known to travel long distances in search of these resources.
9. **Social behavior**: Elephants live in complex societies with a strict hierarchy, and have been observed showing empathy, cooperation, and self-awareness.
10. **Specialized senses**: Elephants have a keen sense of smell and hearing, and are able to detect seismic vibrations through their feet, which helps them to detect watering holes and other important resources.

Additionally, elephants have adaptations that help them to conserve water, such as:

1. **Producing very dry dung**: Which helps to conserve water and reduce water loss.
2. **Ability to go without water**: For extended periods of time, allowing them to survive in areas with limited water resources.

These adaptations enable elephants to thrive in a variety of environments, from savannas and grasslands to forests and deserts.

ADAPTATION SUMMARY: Trunk, Tusks, Ears, Skin, Feet
